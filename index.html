By the night as it envelops (1) And by the day as it appears (2) And by [His] creation of the male and the female (3) Indeed, your efforts are diverse (4)

This is an oath from Allah by the time in which the actions of the servants occur, based on their differing states. So He said: {By the night as it envelops} [meaning: it covers] the creation with its darkness, so everyone settles in their dwelling and home, and the servants rest from their toil and hardship. {And by the day as it appears} to the creation, so they are illuminated by its light and disperse for their livelihoods and interests. {And by [His] creation of the male and the female} If the word "ma" (what/His) is a relative pronoun, then it is an oath by His noble Self, described as being the Creator of the male and female. If it is a gerund, then it is an oath by His very act of creating the male and female, and the perfection of His wisdom in doing so, for He created from every species of animal that He wishes to remain, a male and a female, so that the species may continue and not perish. And He drew each of them to the other with the chain of desire, and made each of them suitable for the other. So blessed is Allah, the best of creators.

And His statement: {Indeed, your efforts are diverse} This is the subject of the oath. Meaning: indeed, your efforts, O accountable ones, are widely divergent. This is due to the variation in the actions themselves, their quantity, the energy put into them, and according to the intended goal of those actions. Is it the Countenance of Allah, the Most High, the Everlasting? If so, the effort remains by His permanence, and its doer benefits from it. Or is it a transient, perishing goal, so the effort is nullified by its nullification and vanishes with its vanishing? And this is the case for every deed intended for other than the Countenance of Allah, the Most High, in this description. For this reason, Allah, the Most High, distinguished between the workers and described their deeds.

As for he who gives and fears Allah (5) And believes in the best [reward] (6) We will ease him toward ease (7)

{As for he who gives} [meaning] what he has been commanded of financial acts of worship, such as Zakat, expiations, and various expenditures and charities, and physical acts of worship, such as Salah, fasting, and their like, and those that combine both, such as Hajj and Umrah, and their like. {and has Taqwa (fears Allah)} concerning what He has forbidden, of prohibitions and sins of all kinds.

{And believes in the best [reward]} meaning: he believes in "La ilaha illallah" (There is no god worthy of worship except Allah) and all that it implies of religious beliefs, and what results from it of reward in the Hereafter.

{We will ease him toward ease} meaning: We will make his affair easy for him, and make every good deed easy for him to perform, and make leaving every evil deed easy for him, because he came with the means of facilitation, so Allah facilitated it for him.

But as for he who is stingy and considers himself self-sufficient (8) And denies the best [reward] (9) We will ease him toward hardship (10) And his wealth will not avail him when he plummets [into Hell] (11)

{But as for he who is stingy} with what he has been commanded, so he abandons obligatory and recommended spending, and his soul does not permit him to expend what Allah has obliged upon him. {and considers himself self-sufficient} from Allah, so he abandons servitude to Him, and does not see himself in dire need of his Lord, who has no salvation, no success, and no felicity for him except by Him being his beloved and his object of worship, whom he intends and turns to. {And denies the best [reward]} meaning: what Allah has obliged the servants to believe in from sound creeds. {We will ease him toward hardship} meaning: for the state of hardship and the blameworthy characteristics, such that he is made ready for evil wherever he may be, and the paths of sin are made easy for him. We ask Allah for well-being.

{And his wealth will not avail him} the wealth that made him transgress and caused him to be stingy {when he plummets} and dies, for nothing accompanies him except his righteous deeds. As for his wealth from which he did not give what was obligatory, it will be a source of calamity for him, as he did not send forth anything from it for his Hereafter.

Indeed, upon Us is guidance (12) And indeed, to Us belongs the Hereafter and the first [life] (13) So I have warned you of a Fire that blazes (14) None will [enter to] burn therein except the most wretched (15) Who denied and turned away (16)

{Indeed, upon Us is guidance} meaning: the straight path of guidance leads to Allah and brings one close to His pleasure. As for misguidance, its paths are blocked from reaching Allah; they only lead their follower to severe punishment.

{And indeed, to Us belongs the Hereafter and the first [life]} They are Our dominion and at Our disposal; there is no one who shares with Us in them. So let those who desire seek from Us and let their hope be cut off from the creation. {So I have warned you of a Fire that blazes} meaning, it is kindled and aflame. {None will [enter to] burn therein except the most wretched, Who denied} the truth {and turned away} from the command.

But the most righteous one will be kept away from it (17) He who gives his wealth to purify himself (18) And not having for anyone a favor to be rewarded (19) Except for seeking the Countenance of his Lord, the Most High (20) And he is going to be satisfied (21)

{But the most righteous one will be kept away from it, He who gives his wealth to purify himself} meaning that his intention in spending is the purification of his own soul, and cleansing it from sins and defects, seeking the Countenance of Allah. This indicates that if the recommended spending includes abandoning an obligation, like a debt or a required expense, then it is not legislated. Rather, his giving is rejected by many of the scholars, because he does not purify himself by a recommended act that causes him to miss an obligation.
